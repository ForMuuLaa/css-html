LET THE ADVENTURE BEGIN
Mercedes-Benz Stadium

No trip to Atlanta is complete without a visit to the iconic Mercedes-Benz Stadium, a modern marvel and a hub of excitement right in the heart of the city. Whether you're catching an adrenaline-pumping Atlanta Falcons football game or cheering on Atlanta United in a sea of soccer fans, the energy here is electric. Even when there’s no game, the stadium offers epic behind-the-scenes tours where you can explore locker rooms, walk the field, and admire the unique halo video board—the largest of its kind in the world. Don’t miss grabbing a bite from the fan-favorite concession stands that are surprisingly affordable. It’s not just a stadium—it’s an experience!

Georgia Aquarium

Dive into an underwater wonderland at the Georgia Aquarium, one of the largest and most impressive aquariums in the world! Located in downtown Atlanta, this place is packed with jaw-dropping exhibits that bring you face-to-fin with whale sharks, beluga whales, sea otters, and more. You can walk through a massive glass tunnel surrounded by swimming sea creatures, watch playful dolphins leap in an energetic show, or even book a behind-the-scenes encounter with penguins or sharks if you're feeling bold. It’s fun for all ages—whether you’re a marine life fanatic or just looking for a magical escape for the day.

The High Museum of Art

The High Museum of Art

Step into a world of creativity and inspiration at the High Museum of Art, Atlanta's premier destination for art enthusiasts and casual visitors alike. Boasting a collection of over 20,000 works, the museum showcases everything from classic European paintings to contemporary masterpieces, African art, and captivating photography. The architectural brilliance of Richard Meier and Renzo Piano adds to the museum's allure, making it a visual treat inside and out. Don't miss the engaging special exhibitions, such as the recent "Giants: Art from the Dean Collection of Swizz Beatz and Alicia Keys," which highlighted contemporary Black artists . Whether you're exploring solo, with friends, or family, the High offers interactive programs, family-friendly activities, and even Friday night events that blend art with live music and entertainment. It's not just a museum—it's an experience that celebrates art in all its forms!​
